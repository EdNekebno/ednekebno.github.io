The ZX Spectrum is my all-time favorite retro computer. After the rives.io Game Jam, I got curious how much it could do and whether it could support a ZX Spectrum Emulator.

Being able to run ZX Spectrum games opens up the possibilities to use rives features such as playback and score verification with games that can be 30-40 years old!

It turns out that it is very possible to do. Your game controller can emulate a Kempston Joystick or keys can be remapped. The only thing that turns out to be elusive is good 128k AY sound chip sound - perhaps it's a step too far for a limited system, perhaps I'll come back another time and solve it. At the moment it makes sound in 128k mode, but it's not great. 48k mode is good. As is everything else.

The emulator loads .z80 or .tap files, but .z80 is preferable from a user experience perspective.
